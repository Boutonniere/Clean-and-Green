CONSERVE THE NATURE

Healthy ecosystems maintain our soil, clean our air, control the climate, recycle nutrients, and give us food. They offer resources. Humans rely heavily on nature, which is a phrase that includes both living things and the factors that shape the natural world, including the weather, mountains, oceans, and landscapes. Even city inhabitants in contemporary skyscrapers require nature's gifts of air to breathe, water to drink, and food to consume in order to survive. While ready-to-eat foods and bottled water are available at supermarkets, they were not made there. Certain fruits and vegetables, for instance, can only be grown in tropical regions and must travel the world in chilled shipping containers to reach your local store at the perfect time of ripeness. As humans do not now possess the capacity to produce significant quantities of water in a laboratory, all drinking water ultimately originates from a natural source.
